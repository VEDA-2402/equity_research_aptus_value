## Model Structure

**Income Statement Model (FY20–FY29E)**
- 5 years historical + 5 years projected
- Assumption-driven: Loan Book Growth, NIM, Cost-to-Income, Credit Cost, Tax Rate
- PAT growing from ₹751 Cr (FY24) → ₹1,806 Cr (FY29E) at ~19% CAGR

**Balance Sheet Model**
- Borrowings driven by Equity Funding % assumption
- Reserves roll forward via PAT − Dividends
- Balance check = 0 across all projection years

**DDM Valuation**
- Cost of Equity: 11.78% (CAPM: Rf 7.1% + β 0.85 × ERP 5.5%)
- Terminal Growth: 4.5% (GDP-linked)
- DDM gives ₹49 — deliberately conservative; Aptus retains ~75% of earnings at 17%+ ROE, which DDM understates

**Comparable Company Analysis**
- Peers: Aavas Financiers, Home First Finance, Five Star Business Finance, Repco Home Finance
- Peer median P/BV: 2.7x | Peer median P/E: 17.8x
- Justified P/BV (ROE/Ke): 1.5x — market prices in significant franchise premium

## Investment Thesis

**Why BUY:**
- India's housing deficit and PMAY tailwinds provide a decade-long structural growth runway
- Best-in-class ROA of 6.1% and NIM of 8.9% vs peer average ROA of 4.6% — pricing power from informal-borrower segment
- Gross NPA 1.1% (lowest in class), D/E 1.7x vs sector 5–6x — significant headroom for ROE expansion without leverage risk

**Key Risks:**
- ~70% geographic concentration in Tamil Nadu
- Floating-rate borrowings expose NIM to rate cycle risk
- Self-employed informal segment is procyclical — credit cost buffer is thin at 0.5%
